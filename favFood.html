AGLIO E OLIO

the pinnacle of italian food

    ingredients
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup solive oil
  • 4 to 6 cloves of thinly sliced garlic
  • chili flakes
  • parsley
  • a lot of parmesean
  • a half a lemon, room temperature
yeah sure it sounds super complicated, but trust me its super simple
  1. fresh pasta! make a mound of all your pasta and create a little well in the center
  2. crack all three of your eggs into the center
  3. beat the eggs with a fork and gradually beat the flour in
  4. once its all incorporated , knead for five minutes until a smooth, cohesive, and firm dough forms
  5. let the dough rest for up to thirty minutes, and at this point the dough can be stored in a fridge for up to 24 hours
  6. once it is done resting, divide the dough in half and roll one half of it out into a large sheet, aim for a 14 in by 16 in rectangle
  7. alternatively, use a pasta roller
  8. once your pasta is nice and flat, cut it and shape it into whatever shape you want.
    aglio e olio is best with some linguini, so don't flatten your pasta too much and cut out strips of about a tenth of an inch
  9. dust and toss the pasta in some flour
  10. on the stove, get the olive oil nice and hot. once the oil starts shimmering, dump in the garlic and sautee until toasty brown
  11. in a bot of boiling water dump in your pasta. becasue this is fresh pasta, we don't need to boil it as long. about two minutes will suffice for al dente pasta
  12. once the pasta is cooked al dente (or however you prefer), grab the pasta and dump it into the oil and garlic with some of the pasta water
  13. throw in your chili flakes, finely chopped parsley, and squeeze in the lemon juice
  14. toss the pasta (or stir or you're not confident in your tossing abilities
  15. enjoy a dish made of a handful of simple ingredients, and embodies when the whole is more than the sum of its parts
